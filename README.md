# Daily Cash Flow Planner

A simple weekly cash flow forecasting template for small businesses and freelancers. Fill in expected inflows and outflows for the week ahead, and the sheet shows you where your cash will stand at the end of every day — so you can spot a shortfall before it hits.

Part of the **Clarity Cash Flow · CashFlowBuddy Program**.

## What's in this repo

- `CashFlowBuddy_Daily Cash Flow Planner_template updated 2026-05-09.xlsx` — the template, with two sheets:
  - **Daily Cash Flow Planner** — the working planner (Monday → Sunday, plus a Weekly Total column)
  - **How to Use** — step-by-step instructions

## How it works

You enter two things:
1. **Starting Cash Balance** — today's available bank balance
2. **Money coming in / going out** for each day of the week (client payments, payroll, rent, vendors, subscriptions, etc.)

The sheet calculates:
- **Total Inflows** and **Total Outflows** per day
- **Net Cash Flow** (Inflows − Outflows) per day, color-coded green/red
- **Ending Daily Balance** — your projected bank balance at the end of each day, carried forward through the week
- **Weekly Total** column summarizing the full week

## Quick start

1. Download the `.xlsx` file.
2. Open it in Excel, Google Sheets, or any spreadsheet app that supports `.xlsx`.
3. Fill in the **yellow cells only** — everything else is calculated for you.
4. Enter the Monday date next to "Week of:" and your current bank balance next to "Starting Cash Balance:".
5. Add expected inflows and outflows in the day they'll actually hit your account.
6. Watch the **Ending Daily Balance** row — if any day goes negative, address it before it happens.

## Tips

- Be **conservative on inflows** (only count what you're confident will arrive) and **aggressive on outflows** (include everything).
- Save a fresh copy each Monday so you can compare forecast to actuals.
- The Net Cash Flow row turns red on negative days — a quick visual of which days drain cash.
